    Katt Williams Facts

  • He grew up in Cincinnati, Ohio.
  • Katt was 13 years old when he emancipated himself from his parents and moved to Florida. He worked as a street vendor to support himself.
  • Katt Williams made his theatrical film debut as Money Mike in the stoner comedy film, Friday After Next, in 2002.
  • He provided vocals to the character of Seamus, a dim-witted, clumsy carrier pigeon, in the 2010 spy comedy film, Cats & Dogs: The Revenge of Kitty Galore.
  • Alongside Owen Wilson, Ed Helms, and others, Katt Williams co-starred in the 2017 comedy film, Father Figures (known as Who’s Your Daddy?), in which he appeared as an unnamed hitchhiker.
  • In March 2018, he was cast as Willie in an episode titled Alligator Man of the FX surreal comedy-drama series, Atlanta. This performance won him a Primetime Emmy Award for “Outstanding Guest Actor in a Comedy Series”.
  • Williams has been friends with comedian, rapper, and actor Nick Cannon.
