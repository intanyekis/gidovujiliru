Is Kissing Booth 4 on Netflix's Horizon? Cast Teases Future

As of now, the prospect of “The Kissing Booth 4” appearing on Netflix remains uncertain. While the cast has expressed interest in exploring their characters’ futures and fans continue to show enthusiasm for the series, there has been no official confirmation from Netflix or the series’ director, Vince Marcello, about a fourth installment.

The Kissing Booth Series

Netflix’s “The Kissing Booth” has emerged as a blockbuster sensation, significantly boosting the careers of its young stars Joey King, Jacob Elordi, and Joel Courtney.

This romantic comedy trilogy, adapted from the novels of Beth Reekles, chronicles the lives of teenagers Elle and Noah.

Their journey, laden with the intricacies of friendships and romantic entanglements, strikingly captures the essence of adolescence.

Elle, portrayed by Joey King, finds herself entangled in a romantic liaison with Noah, played by Jacob Elordi while navigating the dynamics of her longstanding friendship with Lee, enacted by Joel Courtney.

This interplay of relationships forms the crux of the series, unfolding across three films that collectively explore the bittersweet nuances of growing up.

Speculations About The Kissing Booth 4

With the trilogy reaching its conclusion in the third film, speculation abounds regarding the potential for a fourth installment.

While the original book series has come to an end, the movies have occasionally strayed into new narrative territories, fueling discussions among fans about possible future directions.

Despite the deviation from its literary roots, the films have resonated profoundly with the audience, albeit receiving mixed reviews from critics.

The possibility of “The Kissing Booth 4” materializing remains uncertain. As of now, there is no official announcement from Netflix or director Vince Marcello about continuing the series.

However, an intriguing element that keeps hopes alive is the presence of an alternate ending, suggesting unexplored avenues for the characters’ stories.

This creative choice leaves a window open for potentially revisiting and expanding the universe of Elle and Noah, albeit the path forward is laden with challenges, including the need for fresh source material.

Netflix’s Stance on “The Kissing Booth 4” and Future Prospects

Currently, there is no official news regarding the development of “The Kissing Booth 4.” Neither Netflix nor Vince Marcello, the director of the series, have confirmed any plans to add another chapter to this popular franchise.

One key issue is the series’ narrative conclusion, which seemed to neatly tie up the characters’ stories. Additionally, the reception of the previous films, particularly from the cast, plays a significant role.

Notably, Jacob Elordi, who plays Noah, has expressed dissatisfaction with his involvement in the franchise, even considering quitting acting at one point.

This sentiment could influence Netflix’s decision-making process, considering the importance of maintaining a cohesive original cast for the continuity of the series.

Critics vs. Audience Reception

“The Kissing Booth” films, while achieving immense popularity among viewers, have consistently received lukewarm responses from critics.

The initial movie released in 2018 garnered a modest 15% rating on Rotten Tomatoes, with its sequels faring only slightly better. Despite this, the series has been a commercial success for Netflix.

Netflix’s primary focus is on its subscription base, and in this regard, “The Kissing Booth” has excelled, drawing significant viewer numbers with minimal marketing investment.

This strategy aligns with Netflix’s broader approach of allowing viewers to discover original films organically. The success story of Beth Reekles, who penned the original books as a teenager, adds an inspiring layer to the series’ narrative.

Joey King on “The Kissing Booth 4”

Joey King, who has become synonymous with the character Elle, shared her thoughts on the conclusion of the “Kissing Booth” trilogy and the potential for a fourth movie.

While she appreciates how the story was wrapped up, King expressed a willingness to revisit her character.

She also hinted at the possibility of Netflix taking “The Kissing Booth” in new directions, akin to how other popular series have spawned spin-offs or serialized adaptations.

However, she also acknowledges that the story seems to have reached its natural conclusion, thus tempering expectations for a direct sequel.

Joel King told the New York Times “Watching this story be wrapped up so nicely in like a beautiful bow, I think it would be a little hard to come back after that. We made this ending exactly what I think it needed to be. Selfishly, do I want to play Elle again? Absolutely. But I think that the story is on its final chapter.”

Potential Directions for The Kissing Booth 4

The cast of “The Kissing Booth 3” has pondered the future of their characters, with Joey King envisioning Elle in her 40s, potentially as a mother.

Joel Courtney, who plays Lee, echoed this sentiment, suggesting a continuation where the characters are parents, imparting wisdom to the next generation.

Their ideas reflect a desire to see the characters evolve beyond their teenage years, exploring new life stages and challenges.

Jacob Elordi, on the other hand, proposed a more radical leap into the future, imagining the characters in a nursing home.

This idea presents a stark contrast to the series’ current timeline, offering a unique perspective on the characters’ life journeys.

The cast’s diverse visions for a potential “The Kissing Booth 4” demonstrate the breadth of possibilities that could be explored, should the franchise continue.
