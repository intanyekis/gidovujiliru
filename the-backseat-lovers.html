Overall Meaning

In "Watch Your Mouth," The Backseat Lovers touch on the topic of a failed relationship in which one party has moved on and the other has not. The singer seems to be hurt and bitter that their ex-partner has a new job, new hair, and a new guy, and wonders if they care about the singer's feelings at all. The lyrics "Do you think I care / About your new job and your new hair / Do you think I cry / 'Bout your new friends and your new guy / I do" effectively convey the singer's sense of betrayal and abandonment.


The chorus of the song serves as a warning to the ex-partner, reminding them to watch their tone and watch their mouth when speaking to the singer, who seems to have become emotionally unstable. The line "Slide a drink across the table / Watch it fall onto the ground / Keep your distance she's unstable / Watch your tone and watch your mouth" creates a sense of tension and unease, implying that the singer may lash out if provoked.


The final verse of the song speaks to the singer's feelings of inadequacy and worthlessness in the relationship, with lines like "Was I just a waste of time" and "All you do is scratch and bite." The repetition of the phrase "Take my love" at the end of the song implies that the singer is struggling to let go of their feelings for their ex-partner, even though it seems like they have already moved on.


Overall, "Watch Your Mouth" is a powerful and emotional song that effectively captures the pain and heartbreak that can come with the end of a relationship.
